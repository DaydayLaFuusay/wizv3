The Complexity of Identifying Cybersecurity Incident Response Threats

Identifying cybersecurity incident response threats in the context of ISO/SAE 21434:2021 is a complex task. This is due to the intricate nature of cyber threats that are constantly evolving and becoming more sophisticated. Additionally, the interconnectedness of IoT devices in the automotive industry increases the potential attack surface, making it more challenging for both humans and technologies to spot threats.

*"The complexity of the automotive industry’s supply chain and the use of third-party components within software makes it challenging to ensure the security of all elements within the system."* - **Dr. David Ward, Head of Functional Safety, HORIBA MIRA**

Red Flags for Cybersecurity Incident Response Threats

Here are some red flags that can help in identifying potential cybersecurity incident response threats:

  • 1 Unusual network traffic patterns or increased network activity.
  • 2 Unauthorized attempts to access systems or data.
  • 3 Unexpected system reboots or shutdowns.
  • 4 Unusual activity on user accounts, especially during off-hours.
  • 5 Numerous failed login attempts.
  • 6 Unexpected changes in system configurations.
  • 7 Presence of unknown or unapproved software or files.
  • 8 Slow or impaired system performance.

Checklist to Avoid Cybersecurity Incident Response Threats

Follow this checklist to mitigate the risk of cybersecurity incident response threats:

  • 1 Implement a robust cybersecurity incident response plan.
  • 2 Regularly monitor systems and networks for unusual activity.
  • 3 Conduct regular cybersecurity audits and assessments.
  • 4 Train staff on cybersecurity best practices and how to identify potential threats.
  • 5 Keep all systems, software, and devices updated with the latest security patches.
